# Meal Prep Day Workflow

Read this when helping Noah plan or execute his Sunday meal prep session.

## Timing

Sunday meal prep is a recurring task, typically morning (before 10 AM is prime time). Budget 6–8 blocks (1.5–2 hours) depending on complexity. This aligns with the time-blocking skill.

## Mise en Place (Everything in Its Place)

Before any cooking starts:
1. **Start with a clean kitchen.** Clear counters, empty dishwasher/drying rack.
2. **Read all recipes fully.** Check for overnight marination, thaw times, or anything that needs advance prep.
3. **Gather everything.** Ingredients, pots, pans, bowls, utensils, cutting boards, storage containers.
4. **Prep all ingredients first.** Dice, chop, grate, measure, drain cans. Group ingredients that go in together.
5. **Set up workspace.** Damp towel under cutting board, garbage bowl nearby, basin of hot soapy water in sink, dry towel for hands.

## Order of Operations

Start with what takes longest. Work backward from "everything done at roughly the same time."

1. **Longest cook time first.** If something needs 45 min in the oven, start that before anything else.
2. **While that cooks, prep the next thing.** Use oven/simmer time to chop, measure, or assemble cold items.
3. **Hot items before cold.** Hot food needs time to cool before going into containers.
4. **Grains and legumes early.** Rice, quinoa, lentils — set them and forget them while you work on other things.
5. **Proteins next.** Tofu pressing/baking, eggs, tuna salad mixing.
6. **Vegetables and salads last.** These stay freshest when prepped later.
7. **Sauces and dressings at the end.** Quick to make, can be portioned into small containers.

## Clean As You Go

- Wash tools between uses (especially cutting boards after different ingredients).
- Wipe counters between prep stages.
- Load dishwasher as you go — don't let it pile up.
- By the time food is cooling, the kitchen should be mostly clean.

## Storage and Portioning

- Use glass containers when possible (Pyrex or similar). They stack well and reheat better.
- **Label containers** with contents and date.
- **Portion for protein goals.** Each container/meal should hit roughly 25–35g protein to stay on track for 100g daily (accounting for breakfast protein shake and snacks).
- Fridge meals keep 3–4 days. If prepping for the full week, freeze Thu–Sun portions.
- Air fryer reheating > microwave for most things.

## Batch-Friendly Protein Ideas

These scale well for weekly prep:
- Baked tofu (marinated, cubed, baked at 400°F)
- Hard-boiled eggs (batch of 6–8)
- Seasoned chickpeas or black beans
- Tuna salad (mix day-of to keep fresh)
- Soy curls (rehydrate + season in bulk)
- Impossible meat crumbles (taco/bowl seasoning)
- Big pot of lentils (dal, soup, or grain bowl base)
