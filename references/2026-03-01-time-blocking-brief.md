## What is Time Blocking?

Time blocking is a time management method where you break your day into specific time blocks, each dedicated to a particular task, activity, or appointment. You schedule your day in advance and allocate specific time slots for various activities.

### Time Blocking vs. Timeboxing

| Time Blocking | Timeboxing |
|---------------|------------|
| Schedule chunks for specific tasks | Set fixed time limit for tasks |
| Complete the task in that slot | Move on whether finished or not |
| Creates structured approach | Creates urgency and focus |

## The Science Behind Time Blocking

### Pickle Jar Theory
Prioritize "big rocks" (important tasks) first, then fill gaps with smaller pebbles, sand, and water. Time blocking lets you allocate "big rocks" to specific slots.

### Parkinson's Law
Work expands to fill time available. Time blocking creates fixed windows that prevent tasks from ballooning.

### Task Switch Cost
Switching between tasks costs productivity — time blocking encourages single-task focus. Cal Newport advocates for "deep work" through time blocking.

### Procrastination Stats
94% of people say procrastination negatively affects their happiness (APA survey).

## Types of Time Blocking

### 1. Day Theming
Assign each day of the week to a specific theme (e.g., "Marketing Monday," "Deep Work Friday")

### 2. Task Batching
Group similar tasks together to reduce context switching and maintain flow state

### 3. Timeboxing
Set fixed time limits for tasks — move on when time is up

### 4. Pomodoro Technique
25-minute work intervals + 5-minute breaks. Every 4th block = 15-30 min break.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not blocking your whole day** — Partial blocking is less effective
2. **Not prioritizing first** — Block important tasks, not just urgent ones
3. **Overcommitting** — Be realistic about task duration
4. **No wiggle room** — Leave buffer time for unexpected tasks

---

## 8 Steps to Get Started

1. **Identify tasks and priorities** — List everything, then rank by importance
2. **Review your calendar** — Know existing commitments first
3. **Allocate time slots** — Estimate realistically based on energy levels
4. **Avoid overloading** — Don't back-to-back everything
5. **Set clear expectations** — Communicate availability to others
6. **Minimize distractions** — Turn off notifications, close unnecessary tabs
7. **Review and adjust** — Regularly check if blocks are working
8. **Use the right tool** — Consider AI-powered calendar apps
